Living Waredrobe

This wardrobe has been created during my second semester of Wood Design.

 

The wardrobe is Designed to be very interactive. The main goal for this piece of furniture was to have a seriously looking classical piece of furniture for the entrance area with some special features for gym training. This would give the entrance are another purpose of use in order to pursue a more effective use of space. The end result is a wall mounted wardrobe that can be adjusted with a self wedging system which gives extra strength and support for body weight. The triangular shaped racks are design simply enough to serve different needs like hanging cloth, sitting and doing pull ups or push ups. The wedging cleat system could easily be used with further extensions. Such as shelves, key holders, shoe storage or as a skateboard holder.

Coffee Table

This whole assembly is a harmony of elements. The heavy and massive slab is supported by elegant and strong metal rebar. The rebar is bent with a soft radius and angled both in a V shape and to the outside of the table. This gives the legs a very light and uplifting look which makes the Coffee Table seem like it it floating with balanced energies. This balance of opposing elements gives the table a very well rounded fell and an attractive look.

 

The Coffe Table consists of a big heavy slab of a sequoia tree and 1cm thick rebar which. No additional material or screws have been used to build this very simple table.

Staking Bole

I made these bowl as part of my fist semester of studying Wood Design.

They are made to be use for storage of various things in for example in the kitchen. The interesting detail is that you can actually stack these bowls on top of each other when they are filled. At the same time you can stack inside each other when they are empty which is way more space saving when you want to store the bowls. This only possible though the precise dimensions in with and length and though angled the sidewalls.

The final product has a clean and simple look. The assembly of multiple bowls is verry diverse and playful. The look of multiple bowl also reflects this diverse and distractive picture.

Tree Hook

This is a shoe horn keeper I made from a piece of birch with bark. The Idea was, to make it look like a piece of the trees bark had grown out of the wall. And it was supposed to keep a wooden shoehorn. My dream was to work with strong magnets and have no visible connection at all. Due to the irregular shape of both the bark and the shoe horn I decided to go a more simple mechanic fool prove way. The shoe horn hooks through the loop to secure it from tipping and is being held up by a little foot of birch bark. At the end I think I made a very functional shoehorn holder with a calm  and natural look.

Key Board

This key hanger is one of my first pieces. It has a very intelligent design and servers multiple purposes. You can hook your keys very simply using the hidden magnets. Also this key hanger has a little slot where you can put your latest post cards and see them whenever you leave the house. You can also stick the letter you needed to bring to the post office in the little slot so you don't forget next time. At last you have a neat spot to put everything else that is cluttering up your entrance area like your sunglasses or your wallet. All is supported by the little ledge in the front. All of these functions are crammed into this little beautifully simple Key Board. I is even possible to create this Key Board in a custom length for more or less keys without changes in the manufacturing process.
